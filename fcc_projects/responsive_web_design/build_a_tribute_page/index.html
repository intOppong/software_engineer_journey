A Tribute

To all those who inspire & motivate me everyday on my journey to become a Software Engineer.

Text

Quincy Larson

Quincy is the founder of the open source community freecodecamp that helps you learn to code. He was a school director and teacher before making a career change to software engineering after discovering it’s power to automate tasks in the school. He’s focused on building the biggest online system that helps people with no coding background learn to code & get jobs in the industry.That’s exactly what freecodecamp has done since it’s inception with over 200,000 daily visitors. His story about learning to code is just pure inspiration. You can check it out here.

“My goal was to help build the resource that I wished had existed when I first started learning to code. I believed deeply in universal access to education, and publicly committed that freeCodeCamp would always be 100% free”

Scott Young

Scott is the one who taught me that you can do/learn anything if you put your mind to it & apply the right methods. He’s taken on several challenges to master skills in diverse areas. He completed MIT’s 4-year undergraduate computer science curriculum in 12 months, without taking any classes. He was successful in passing the final exams for 33 classes and completing the required programming projects. You can view his TEDx Talk regarding the challenge here. He also learnt 4 different languages in a year without speaking English. He also did a 30-day challenge to see how much improvement he could make in drawing faces & did a pretty good job at that too.

Haseeb Qureshi

Haseeb is a general partner at MetaStable Capital, a cryptocurrency hedge fund. He’s also a programmer, a writer and a teacher at Bradfield School of Computer Science. But he like to think of himself first as an Effective Altruist. He was formerly a software engineer at Airbnb. Before that He was Director of Product at App Academy. Before that he was a mental coach and wrote a book called The Philosophy of Poker. And before that he was a world-class professional poker player, sponsored by Full Tilt Poker. He wrote a viral article about his job search in the tech space.

“if you work your ass off, you will get there”

P1xt

P1xt is a software engineer, gamer and “geek”. She's written several guides that in a variety of domains to help aspiring developers & engineers learn & achieve their dreams. She’s earned my respect for the work she put in those guides and constantly replying to our questions on the freecodecamp forum as if she had to.

John Sonmez

John is a software consultant who runs the simple programmer youtube channel & website. He’s just an inspiration for aspiring developers.He constantly makes videos advising & answering questions on his youtube channel. He talks about the life of a software engineer not just the programming aspect but the soft skills, physical health, financials among many other things.
